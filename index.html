Research

Neutron stars are one of the most extreme environments in the universe. Their central densities can exceed 1015 g/cm3, making them several times denser than matter that an atomic nucleus. What happens when matter is compressed to such densities is one of the big open questions in modern nuclear- and astro-physics. My research works to connect astrophysical observations of neutron stars with detailed models of the stellar interior, in order to probe the nature of ultra-dense matter.

About me

I am an Assistant Professor of Physics & Astronomy at Swarthmore College, starting in Fall of 2024.
I completed my PhD in Astronomy & Astrophysics at the University of Arizona in 2020. From 2020 to 2024, I was a joint postdoctoral fellow at the Princeton Center for Theoretical Science, Princeton Gravity Initiative, and as a John N. Bahcall fellow at the Institute for Advanced Study.
